Questions, answered.

Everything about Set Your Sunday — what it is, how the engine actually works, and what it takes to ship it rights-clean.

What is Set Your Sunday, in one line?▾

A contextual CTV engine for YouTube Sunday Ticket. Genius Sports performance data casts the week's Top Picks — the projected must-starts — and one ad reskins every day to meet fantasy managers at that day's decision, from waivers to lineup lock. Then Sunday Ticket is how they watch every one of those players actually play.

What makes it "contextual" — what actually changes day to day?▾

The creative template is fixed; the contents recompose. The engine reads live signals — the planning day and the week's projections — and swaps the backdrop, the Top Picks board and the headline so the ad speaks to the exact decision a manager is making that day. Waiver Wednesday reads differently than Sunday-morning lineup lock. Same frame, new message, every day of the week.

Where does the data come from?▾

Genius Sports performance projections. The Top Picks are cast on forward-looking data — who's about to go off this week, not who went off last week — so the same data that tells a manager who to start is what decides who appears in the ad.

Are these real players? What about likeness rights?▾

The concept is built around real stars — that's what makes the start/sit call land. In this demo there's no player likeness; the finished campaign depends on securing rights to real player names, images, and the NFL shield, team logos and uniforms. Those clearances are the top of the wishlist above.

How does the AI assembly work — and is the output brand-safe?▾

Three signals — the Top Picks, the planning day, and the day's headline — composite into a finished :15 spot, with Google Gemini generating the stadium backdrop behind them. The engine never invents new claims or creative: it only recombines pre-approved parts. Brand safety is structural, not a review step — see the approval process.

A new ad every day — doesn't that mean a new approval every day?▾

No. You approve the recipe once, not every meal. We lock a single creative template and pre-clear the entire pool of swappable parts — players, marks and headlines — with Google and the NFL up front. In flight, the engine only assembles from already-approved pieces on that day's data, so there's no day-of sign-off and no bottleneck.

Do we still get to see and approve the actual assets?▾

Yes — nothing runs unseen. Because the engine only recombines pre-approved parts, every possible spot can be rendered and reviewed before the flight, not after. You sign off on the locked template and the full library of players, marks and headlines, and we can show the exact daily variants that library produces. If a variant shouldn't run, you pull the part — not the campaign — and the engine simply stops assembling it.

Where does it run, and what's the investment?▾

Premium, signed-in CTV against a live-NFL audience — Amazon Prime Video & Fire TV as the preferred platform partner. Estimated media is $750K–$1.25MM, flighted across the season and weighted to the days and weeks when lineup decisions peak, with a proposed run of Sept 9–28, 2026 — Weeks 1–3, when leagues draft and lineups lock in.

How is success measured?▾

A Brand Lift Study on purchase intent — survey-based lift in stated intent to subscribe to Sunday Ticket, exposed vs. control. The campaign's job is to move intent among fantasy managers, so that's what it's held to.

Why Sunday Ticket for this audience?▾

Sunday Ticket is a premium buy — until you have something higher-stakes on the line. There are ~24MM fantasy players in the U.S., they over-index hard as heavy NFL viewers, and they need to watch their players, not just the local game. Sunday Ticket is the only way to see every one of the Top Picks — out of market, in Fantasy View.

What would it take to build this for real?▾

Mainly rights and partnerships: real player names and images, the NFL shield / team logos / uniforms, game footage, social & creator amplification, and a client pre-approval pass on every ingredient. Clear those and each day's auto-assembled spot ships rights-clean and on-brand. The full list lives in the wishlist above.
